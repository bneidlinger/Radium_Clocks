Historical Significance

This Linden clock embodies the post-radium era‚Äîwhen manufacturers finally realized that maybe, just maybe, painting radioactive materials on consumer products wasn't the brightest idea. By the 1970s, the industry had transitioned to photoluminescent materials that were about as dangerous as a disco ball.

These "no-spice" specimens serve as important baseline comparisons in the collection, demonstrating what background radiation looks like and proving that functional luminous dials could be achieved without turning workers into walking Geiger counters.
